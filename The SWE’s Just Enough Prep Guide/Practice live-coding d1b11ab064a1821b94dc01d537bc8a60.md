# Practice live-coding

<aside>
<img src="https://www.notion.so/icons/briefcase_blue.svg" alt="https://www.notion.so/icons/briefcase_blue.svg" width="40px" /> **This is Part 6 of the Just Enough Prep Guide.** This workbook will offer some advice for successfully passing live coding tests.

</aside>

All interviews are a kind of live performance, and just like other kinds of live performance, it’s very helpful to rehearse.

When live-coding, you are trying to communicate your problem-solving process at the same time as actually solving the problem. Either of those performances is difficult enough on its own. Doing them together smoothly requires rehearsal.

First, make sure that you have defined your structured **problem-solving process**. (Revisit [that chapter](Define%20your%20recipe%20for%20live%20problem-solving%20dc411ab064a18304bc40010d65038681.md) for a refresher).

Next, prepare to code **without your usual IDE**. Often a very simple online coding environment is used in interviews. So those familiar macros and auto-complete and other dev environment plugins will all be gone.

Now tackle some problems and **record yourself**. For this purpose, focus on just two things:

1. Are you following your problem-solving process? 
2. Are you steadily narrating aloud where you are in your problem-solving process?
3. Can you get at least to an 80% solution while adhering to (1) and (2)?

By “80% solution,” we mean here either: 

- a solution that works for most but not all cases, or
- a solution that doesn’t work quite yet, but it would be very clear to any reasonable interviewer that with a little more implementation time it would work.

Now watch at least part of your solution video. Performance improvement opportunities will probably be blatantly obvious.

Rinse and repeat until most of the cringe is gone. (Easier said than done, we know — but even a small amount of practice like this will put you far ahead of not doing it at all).
