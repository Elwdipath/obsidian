# Define your recipe for live problem-solving

<aside>
<img src="https://www.notion.so/icons/briefcase_blue.svg" alt="https://www.notion.so/icons/briefcase_blue.svg" width="40px" /> **This is Part 5 of the Just Enough Prep Guide.** This workbook will help you refine your thought process for solving technical problems.

</aside>

In [What are hiring managers looking for?](What%20are%20hiring%20managers%20looking%20for%200ef11ab064a1825e9e4f81e7c456bf0b.md), one of the key points was:

> *solving challenging problems with a systematic problem-solving approach*
> 

The **anti**-pattern that all teams want to avoid is “diving in without thinking ahead and without much process, thus taking longer and making a mess.”

It’s critical that you demonstrate that you are not that person.

To do so, it’s very helpful to explicitly define your own structured approach (or “recipe”) for live problem-solving.

## Sample Recipe

1. Reflect back the problem statement to the interviewer.
    1. Verify with at least one example of input → output.
2. Identify the overall problem pattern.
3. Discuss possible strategies.
4. Select and sketch out an algorithm in pseudocode.
5. Prepare one or more test cases from the original example(s) in (1).
6. Write a skeleton of the implementation.
7. Fill in the skeleton.
8. Verify the solution.
9. [optional] Optimize the solution.
    1. Basically return to (3).

You may not want to do exactly these steps in exactly this order.  However, you should have clearly defined steps for the types of problems you are likely to be asked to solve.

## Your turn 👇

<aside>
<img src="https://www.notion.so/icons/gym_blue.svg" alt="https://www.notion.so/icons/gym_blue.svg" width="40px" /> **Record yourself working through a challenging problem and develop your own live-problem solving recipe.** The goal is to develop a consistent format for working through problems so you have a clear plan of action during interviews. Use the recipe above or cook up your own — whatever helps you confidently communicate your technical solutions.
