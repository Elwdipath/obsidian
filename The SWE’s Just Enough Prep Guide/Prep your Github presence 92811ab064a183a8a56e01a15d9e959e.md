# Prep your Github presence

</aside>

It’s surprising how many engineers will put their Github handle onto their resume, then spend zero time making a good experience for visitors.

Upon arrival, engineering managers will often just see a bunch of casually-forked repos, half-finished tutorials, perhaps some work from student days, perhaps some other half-baked / empty repos.  Poking around potentially promising repos is often low-yield because their READMEs are empty, or just left as generated boilerplate.

It’s an opportunity wasted.

Here are some easy ways to make your Github useful for visitors:

1. Make an overall [Github profile README](https://docs.github.com/en/account-and-profile/setting-up-and-managing-your-github-profile/customizing-your-profile/managing-your-profile-readme) that at least matches the “who am I” header area of your resume.
2. [Pin the good repos](https://docs.github.com/en/account-and-profile/setting-up-and-managing-your-github-profile/customizing-your-profile/pinning-items-to-your-profile).
3. For each good repo, make sure the README is [professionally formatted and informative](https://github.com/matiassingers/awesome-readme).

## A dolled-up Github landing page

![image (21).png](Prep%20your%20Github%20presence/image_(21).png)

This landing page summarizes who Tobias Leinns, what his specialties are, what topics he is especially passionate about. Tobias went above and beyond by adding in his work availability as well as his contact information. The last two are not always required, but the easier you make it for a hiring manager to get to know you, the more inclined they will be to do so.

You can see that he has a good number of repositories pinned, many of them within the topic areas that he told us he was passionate about in his landing page. His repos are also fully fledged out with thorough and easy to understand READMEs (no boilerplate code).

![Screen Shot 2022-09-28 at 5.43.48 PM.png](Prep%20your%20Github%20presence/Screen_Shot_2022-09-28_at_5.43.48_PM.png)

Completing your Github profile with a header, repositories and READMEs demonstrates that you care enough to make the hiring managers job easy. It also shows attention to detail and an ability to sell yourself — all of which signals that you would be a great addition to their team.
