- From the Ruins of Empire- Pankaj Mishra
	- `"From the Ruins of Empire" by Pankaj Mishra explores the intellectual movements in Asia during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, focusing on key figures like Jamal al-Din al-Afghani, Liang Qichao, and Rabindranath Tagore. The book argues that these thinkers sought to modernize their societies while retaining their cultural identities, ultimately contributing to Asia's resurgence in the global landscape.`
- The World of Yesterday- Stefan Zweig
	- ``
	  "The World of Yesterday" is a memoir by Stefan Zweig that reflects on his life in pre-war Vienna and the cultural richness of the Austro-Hungarian Empire. It captures the sense of security and optimism before World War I, while also detailing the subsequent turmoil and the rise of extremist ideologies that led to his exile and the eventual devastation of Europe during World War II.

- Travelers in the Third Reich - Julia Boyd
	  - `"Travelers in the Third Reich" by Julia Boyd explores the rise of the Nazi regime in Germany from 1919 to 1945 through the firsthand accounts of various visitors, including tourists, diplomats, and artists. The book highlights the confusion and moral dilemmas faced by these individuals as they witnessed the unfolding events, providing a unique perspective on how ordinary people experienced life in Nazi Germany.`
